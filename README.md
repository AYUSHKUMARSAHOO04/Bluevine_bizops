# Overview

This repository contains my submission for the **Business Operations Analyst Take-Home Assessment**.

The objective was to evaluate whether a newly launched payments product is likely to recover its **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** within **180 days**, based on the first three months of customer profitability data.

The assignment combines **business analysis, SQL, Excel modeling, financial projections, and executive communication** to generate actionable recommendations for leadership.

---

# Business Objective

Determine whether the Payments product is on track to recover acquisition costs within **6 months** by:

- Calculating Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Analyzing customer profitability
- Building 6-month payback projections
- Identifying operational risks
- Recommending strategic actions

### Projection Modeling

Two projection approaches were developed:

**Scenario A : Conservative**

- Flat historical run-rate projection

**Scenario B : Optimistic**

- Mature customer run-rate projection

These approaches provide a realistic range of expected CAC recovery while acknowledging the limited observation window.

# Key Findings

- Customer Acquisition Cost varies significantly across acquisition channels.
- Google demonstrates the strongest projected CAC recovery.
- Facebook exhibits the weakest recovery under conservative assumptions.
- Approximately one-third of customers have generated no profit to date.
- Customer profitability is highly concentrated among a relatively small segment of users.
- Current evidence is insufficient to confirm or reject 180-day CAC recovery because no customer has yet reached the full observation period.

---

# Recommendations

### Immediate

- Build a cohort-based CAC Payback Dashboard
- Investigate zero-profit customers
- Validate FundCo acquisition economics

### Medium-Term

- Extend customer observation period
- Conduct channel budget reallocation experiments
- Investigate declining cohort quality
- Implement churn tracking

### Long-Term

- Monitor CAC Payback monthly
- Introduce channel-level spend gates
- Reduce profitability concentration risk
